Procter & Gamble (P&G)

New Product Development Pipeline Report (2010–2025)

Over 15 years, P&G generated ~120,000 ideas.
Only ~4.5% (5,400 products) reached full commercialization.

102,000 Passed Screening (85%)
71,400 Entered Business Analysis (59%)
42,840 Advanced to Development (36%)
21,420 Entered Test Marketing (18%)
5,400 Commercialized (4.5%)
Overall Survival Rate: 4.5%

Why Ideas Fail: Stage-by-Stage Dropout Analysis

Idea Generation → Screening: 18,000 ideas rejected (15%)
Main Reasons: Duplicates, not aligned with brand, technically impossible, or too vague.
P&G uses a standardized checklist — if it doesn’t fit core categories (e.g., laundry, beauty, health), it’s out.
Example: “Tide-flavored toothpaste” — fun, but not on-brand or feasible.
Screening → Business Analysis: 30,600 ideas dropped (30%)
Main Reasons: Market too small, no competitive edge, or low consumer need.
P&G asks: “Will this generate at least $50M in Year 1?” If not, it dies here.
Example: “Eco-friendly diaper for cats” — niche market, low volume.
Business Analysis → Development: 28,560 ideas killed (40%)
Main Reasons: Too expensive to make, supply chain risks, or low profit margin.
P&G requires ≥40% gross margin. High R&D cost + low scalability = rejection.
Example: “Smart diaper with Wi-Fi sensor” — cool tech, but $15/unit cost vs. $1 target.
Development → Test Marketing: 21,420 ideas fail (50%)
Main Reasons: Prototype fails, poor performance, or consumer rejects in lab tests.
P&G runs simulated use tests — if it doesn’t work in real homes, it’s gone.
Example: “Self-heating laundry pod” — melted in hot water during testing.
Test Marketing → Commercialization: 16,020 ideas flop (75%)
Main Reasons: Low trial rate, poor repeat purchase, or beaten by competition in stores.
P&G measures: ≥35% trial and ≥25% repeat in test markets. Most fail repeat.
Example: “Pampers Sleep Spray” — 40% tried it, but only 8% bought again.

Key Takeaways for Students

  • Failure is normal: 95.5% of ideas die — that’s how innovation works.
  • Early kills save money: Screening cuts 15% fast; development kills 40% before big spend.
  • Test marketing is brutal: 75% fail here — customers don’t care about “cool,” only repeat purchase.
  • P&G improved over time: From 2.5% to 5.5% success via data, open innovation, and strict gates.
  • Your SunnyBrew idea? Must survive the same funnel — volume + rigor = success.
