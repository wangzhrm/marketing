Consumer Behavior Quiz

1. What is consumer behavior?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Consumer behavior refers to the process by which ultimate buyers or household consumers make decisions about purchasing products or services.

2. Which of the following is a social factor influencing consumer behavior?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Social factors include external influences like culture, social class, reference groups, family, and opinion leaders.

3. What type of music do high-end restaurants use to encourage customers to stay longer?

Correct Answer: D

Explanation: High-end restaurants use slow-tempo music to create a relaxed atmosphere, encouraging customers to stay longer and order more.

4. What is the first step in the consumer decision process?

Correct Answer: A

Explanation: The consumer decision process begins with recognizing a problem or need that prompts the consumer to take action.

5. What are internal sources in the information search stage?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Internal sources involve a consumer’s mental review of their own experiences and knowledge during the information search stage.

6. What is the evoked set?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: The evoked set is the group of product or brand alternatives a consumer considers when making a purchase decision.

7. What are evaluative criteria?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Evaluative criteria are the specific features, such as quality or price, that a consumer uses to evaluate their options.

8. How can marketers influence the evaluation of alternatives stage?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Marketers can influence this stage by educating consumers about key product attributes and showing why their brand meets those criteria.

9. What does the purchase decision step involve?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: The purchase decision step involves choosing where to buy the product, influenced by factors like delivery options or store convenience.

10. What happens in the purchase act step?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: The purchase act is when the consumer completes the transaction and purchases the chosen product.

11. What is cognitive dissonance?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Cognitive dissonance is the discomfort or imbalance a consumer feels after making a purchase, often due to conflicting thoughts or doubts.

12. What can marketers do to reduce cognitive dissonance?

Correct Answer: A

Explanation: Marketers can reduce cognitive dissonance by providing information that reassures the consumer about their purchase decision.

13. What is involvement in consumer behavior?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Involvement refers to how much interest a consumer has in a product and how important that product is to them.

14. What is routinized response behavior?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Routinized response behavior occurs for low-involvement, frequently purchased products, requiring minimal decision-making effort.

15. When does limited problem solving occur?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Limited problem solving occurs when consumers make less frequent purchases and have limited knowledge or experience with the product.

16. What is extended problem solving?

Correct Answer: A

Explanation: Extended problem solving is used for high-involvement products with significant financial or social risks, requiring thorough decision-making.

17. What determines a person’s social class?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Social class is determined by factors like occupation, education, income or wealth, and possessions.

18. How does social class influence purchasing behavior?

Correct Answer: D

Explanation: Social class influences purchasing behavior through disposable income, brand preferences, and store choices.

19. What are reference groups?

Correct Answer: A

Explanation: Reference groups are individuals or institutions whose opinions guide a consumer’s behavior, values, and purchasing decisions.

20. What is an aspirational reference group?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Aspirational reference groups, like celebrities, are those consumers want to emulate by purchasing items they endorse.

21. What is a non-aspirational reference group?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Non-aspirational reference groups are those consumers do not want to be associated with, leading them to avoid related products.

22. When do reference groups influence purchasing behavior?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Reference groups influence purchasing when the product is visible to others and the consumer is open to their influence.

23. Who are opinion leaders?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Opinion leaders are trendsetters who adopt new products early and influence others through their experiences and opinions.

24. What is the most important determinant of consumer behavior?

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Family is considered the most important determinant of consumer behavior due to its influence on values, preferences, and decisions.

25. What are household lifecycles?

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Household lifecycles refer to life stages like young adulthood, marriage, or childbirth that influence consumer behavior.
