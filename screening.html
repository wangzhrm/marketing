New Product Screening Checklist

Developed from 10+ years of product launches — this checklist helps us quickly filter ideas and avoid costly mistakes. Every “Yes” increases the chance of success. Every “No” triggers a pause or rejection.

Fits Core Brand Identity
Why it matters: Past failures (e.g., “SunnyBrew Protein Shakes”) confused customers. Our brand = premium coffee experience. New products must feel like a natural extension — not a random add-on.
Solves a Real Customer Pain Point
Why it matters: “Matcha Energy Balls” flopped because customers didn’t need another snack. But “15-Second Breakfast Latte” succeeded — it solved long morning lines and hunger in one go.
Feasible with Current Supply Chain
Why it matters: We once approved a “Fresh Coconut Cold Brew” — but couldn’t source coconuts reliably. Now we ask: Can our existing vendors deliver this ingredient at scale?
Gross Margin ≥ 55%
Why it matters: Low-margin items (like $1 pastries) drain profits. High-margin drinks (e.g., oat milk lattes) fund growth. We learned: never launch below 55% margin.
Can Be Made in Under 90 Seconds
Why it matters: Speed = survival in peak hours. A 3-minute “superfood bowl” caused chaos. Now: if it slows the line, it’s out.
Uses Recyclable or Reusable Packaging
Why it matters: Customer backlash killed our plastic bottled iced tea. Now we require zero single-use plastic — aluminum, glass, or reusable only.
Clear Unique Selling Point (USP)
Why it matters: “Just another iced latte” gets ignored. But “World’s First Collagen Cold Brew” went viral. We ask: What makes this undeniably different?
Testable in 1 Store Within 30 Days
Why it matters: Big ideas like “Coffee Subscription Boxes” failed nationally before local testing. Now: if we can’t pilot in one store fast, we don’t proceed.

Rule: An idea must pass at least 6 out of 8 checks to move to Business Analysis.
This checklist has reduced failed launches by 78% since 2019.
